The crossing

Four steps, one transaction

Setu rides Circle's Cross-Chain Transfer Protocol. Your USDC is destroyed on the source chain and re-issued fresh on Arc — never wrapped, never sitting in a bridge's honeypot. A CCTP V2 hook handles the swap the moment it lands.

01

Burn

Your USDC is burned on the source chain. Nothing is locked in a bridge contract for an attacker to drain.

source chain
02

Attest

Circle's attestation service watches the burn and signs it once the source block is final. Fast Transfer signs on soft finality.

circle iris
03

Mint

Native USDC is minted on Arc against that signature — Circle-issued, redeemable 1:1, not a bridge IOU.

arc
04

Swap

The hook fires on arrival, routing the fresh USDC into your chosen asset — all inside the same flow.

arc · hookData
Why it's different

Native, single-shot, ready to use

Native, not wrapped

You get Circle-issued USDC on Arc — not a bridge-minted IOU. No custody pool to drain, no depeg the day a bridge gets exploited.

One transaction

Bridge and swap collapse into a single CCTP V2 hook. No manual claim on the far side, no second signature, no half-finished transfers.

Lands ready on Arc

USDC is gas on Arc. Setu drops you in already able to transact — not stuck hunting for a swap the moment you arrive.
